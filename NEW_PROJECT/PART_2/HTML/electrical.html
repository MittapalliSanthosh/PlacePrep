Ohm's Law
Circuit Theory Basic Law
Medium

Key Concepts:

  • V = IR (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  • Power calculation: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • Series and parallel resistance calculations

Common Applications:

  • Circuit design and analysis
  • Power supply calculations
  • Battery life estimation

Important Notes:

Remember that Ohm's law is valid only for linear resistors at constant temperature. The relationship may not hold for non-linear devices like diodes or under varying temperature conditions.

Kirchhoff's Laws
Circuit Theory Basic Laws
Hard

Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL):

  • Sum of currents entering a node = Sum of currents leaving the node
  • ∑I = 0 at any node
  • Used for analyzing parallel circuits

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL):

  • Sum of voltages around any closed loop = 0
  • ∑V = 0 in any closed loop
  • Used for analyzing series circuits

Application Tips:

When solving complex circuits:

  1. First identify all nodes and loops
  2. Apply KCL at nodes
  3. Apply KVL in loops
  4. Solve the resulting system of equations
